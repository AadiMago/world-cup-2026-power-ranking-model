  This project creates a data-driven model aimed at assessing team strength and indicating possible underdog contenders for the 2026 FIFA World Cup. The goal is to rank the 48 national teams through a custom power score that incorporates various performance aspects, such as recent match results, global standings, historical achievements, and continental accomplishments. The model utilizes SQL to analyze and summarize match data from the last ten games of each team. Match results are transformed into a uniform 3–1–0 points system, and essential performance indicators like points per game and goal difference are computed. These indicators are merged with external information, including FIFA rankings and historical World Cup performance, to generate a thorough evaluation of team strength.

  The power score is determined through a weighted aggregation of various factors: recent performance (points per game), goal difference (indicating dominance), FIFA ranking, World Cup achievements and appearances, as well as continental titles won over the last ten years. A nonlinear transformation is applied to the FIFA ranking element to more effectively differentiate between elite teams and mid-tier teams. This method ensures that both current performance and historical consistency are taken into account.

  Teams are subsequently categorized into tiers according to their power score, ranging from "Favorite" to "Highly Unlikely." These classifications offer a clear way to understand the rankings and highlight possible contenders as well as underdogs.

  The findings are illustrated using Power BI. A bar chart displays teams organized by their power score, while a scatter plot examines the relationship between power score and FIFA ranking. The scatter plot indicates a notable negative correlation between ranking and performance, showcasing teams that either exceed or fall short of expectations based on their ranking. Teams positioned above the trend line are seen as potential dark horse candidates, whereas those below may be experiencing slight underperformance.

  In summary, the analysis indicates that the majority of teams’ performances closely correspond to their FIFA rankings, with relatively few significant exceptions. This implies that the outcomes of the tournament are likely to be influenced predominantly by the higher-ranked teams, although minor variations point to potential surprises.
